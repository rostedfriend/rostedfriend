# Hot & Spicy Maize Bhujja

Ingridients-
250 grams boiled potatoes
2 cups besan (gram flour, approximately 240 to 250 grams)
1 tablespoon hot oil
1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
¾ teaspoon mint powder (optional, but adds flavor)
¼ teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (adjust to taste)
¼ teaspoon black pepper powder
¼ teaspoon chaat masala
¼ teaspoon ginger powder
¼ teaspoon garlic powder
¼ teaspoon garam masala
¼ teaspoon cumin powder
A pinch of hing (asafoetida)

How to make at home!

Boil the Potatoes

Boil whole potatoes in a pressure cooker or pot until just done.

Once cooked, peel the potatoes while still hot/warm and cool them completely.

Grate the potatoes finely using a fine grater.

Prepare the Spice Mix:

Mix the following spice powders: mint powder, Kashmiri red chili powder, black pepper powder, chaat

masala, ginger powder, garlic powder, garam masala, cumin powder, and hing.

Sieve these spice powders along with salt over the grated potatoes.

Add Besan (Gram Flour):

Sieve 2 cups (240 grams) of besan into a separate bowl.

Measure and spread 1½ cups of besan over the potato mixture.

Heat 1 tablespoon of oil until very hot and quickly pour it over the besan.

Incorporate the oil into the flour first using a spoon, then mix everything well with your hands.

The mixture will form a stiff and non-sticky dough. If needed, add more besan gradually (up to 250

grams) to achieve the right consistency.

Shape and Fry:

Fill the dough into a “sev press” (a tool used to make thin strands).

Heat oil in a deep non-stick kadai.

Press out thin strands of the dough from the sev press into the hot oil.

Deep-fry the strands until golden and crispy.

Drain excess oil on paper towels.

###Enjoy!###

Your homemade aloo bhujia is ready! Serve it as a snack or enjoy it with tea.
